Custom Analysis

• Problem statement

In today’s fast-paced world, many people experience skin issues due to modern lifestyle factors, such as lack of sleep and high-stress levels. These factors disrupt the skin's natural barrier, leading to various skin imbalances. According to our iHP survey, 82.1% of respondents believe that skincare is essential. Market research indicates a growing demand for cosmetics, particularly among individuals aged 20 to 40. However, many consumers face budget constraints, finding it challenging to balance affordability with effectiveness. Approximately 44.1% of consumers spend between $28.14 and $70.35 per month on skincare. This is supported by market reports showing that over 63% of women aged 20 to 40 in the Asian market have a monthly cosmetics budget of no more than $50 USD [1].

Figure. Consumers' willingness and budget to buy cosmetics.

• Potential customers & application scene

Recognizing the unmet needs of our customers, we set out to develop skincare products that are both cost-effective and of high quality. Our products are designed to address common skincare issues, be gentle on sensitive skin, and cater to a wide range of individual skin conditions. According to the Asian Consumer Trends Report 2024, individuals aged 20 to 40 make up over 70% of total market spending on cosmetics, highlighting their purchasing power and strong interest in skincare products [2]. Therefore, we have structured our business model as direct-to-consumer (To-C), primarily targeting the young to mid-age population (20-40).

Target audience:

Career Women: This group often faces high work pressure and needs effective skincare solutions to address issues caused by long hours and irregular schedules.

Young Mothers: With their busy family lives and parenting responsibilities, they seek simple, quick, and affordable skincare options.

Health-Conscious Consumers: As healthy lifestyles become more popular, a growing number of consumers are looking for skincare products that are suitable for sensitive skin.

Solution

• Technical feasibility

Our product “Yeastea” has an integrated oscillating system design. We constructed a yeast strain that can produce ceramide and bisabolol. Check project description for more details.

• Minimum viable products

Figure. The minimum viable products—Yeastea.

Our project aims to provide skincare solutions for both the general public and individuals with sensitive skin, with a focus on the young to mid-age population. The benefits of our products include smoothing the skin, reducing inflammation, and strengthening the skin barrier.

To achieve this, we have combined two powerful ingredients—ceramide and bisabolol—into a yeast lysis extract. The growing emphasis on skincare and the rising trend of using yeast essence extract indicate significant market potential. By gathering user feedback in the future, we are confident in developing a product that is both cost-effective and highly beneficial.

• Core competence

1. The all-in-one skincare solution

Unlike traditional yeast extract products, our formula also includes ceramide and α-bisabolol, offering consumers a wide range of skin-repairing benefits in a single solution. Ceramide helps repair the skin barrier and provides deep moisturization, while α-bisabolol delivers anti-inflammatory and soothing effects. Yeast extract enhances hydration and overall skin condition. This all-in-one product addresses the comprehensive skincare needs of modern consumers, including those with sensitive skin, thereby increasing its market appeal.

2. Reducing product toxicity to yeast

We employ a gene oscillation strategy to engineer yeast capable of producing both ceramide and α-bisabolol. Since these substances are toxic to yeast when produced in excess, the oscillation system ensures balance. When the production of one compound reaches a high level, the yeast shifts to producing the other, preventing the accumulation of toxicity from overproduction. This technique optimizes overall production efficiency by minimizing toxicity.

3. Sustainable production

In contrast to conventional plant extraction or chemical synthesis methods, our biosynthetic approach leverages renewable biological resources like yeast, ensuring an eco-friendly process. By fully utilizing yeast lysate, we achieve high resource efficiency and reduce waste, aligning with sustainable practices. This approach meets the growing consumer demand for environmentally friendly products and reflects our team's commitment to environmental protection.

Our initial focus is on the skincare consumers in the southeastern region of China, a key area for skincare product consumption. These individuals represent our potential customer base, signaling strong market potential.

SAM:

As our business expands, we will target the entire Chinese market. The size of China's cosmetics market is about 200 billion CNY, with around 20 billion CNY allocated to skincare products for sensitive skin [3]. We anticipate a greater demand for skincare products like "Yeastea," and aim to reach a target consumer base of 50 million nationwide.

TAM:

To evaluate the maximum market potential of "Yeastea," we considered the global skincare market, which is valued at around 150 billion USD (1 USD = 7.05 CNY as of 9/25/2024). There is increasing global demand for products containing natural ingredients and designed for sensitive skin [4].

In 2021, the market size of China's cosmetics raw materials industry was about 11.074 billion CNY, and it is expected to increase to 11.653 billion CNY in 2022. The East and South China regions are the main consumer areas for cosmetics raw materials, indicating the warehouse and sales advantage. It is expected that by 2025, the scale of China's cosmetics market will reach 579.1 billion CNY [5]. Combined with the trend of the global market, we foresee that "Yeastea" will enter a market that is profitable and has great growth potential. Moreover, the global market size of skincare raw material manufacturers is about 16.706 billion USD, offering a broad selection of suppliers for raw material procurement and supply chain management [6].

Based on this analysis, we have identified a market that is profitable and has great market prospects for skin care products. We are optimistic about the future market potential of "Yeastea”.

Competition Analysis

In the competitive landscape of reparative skincare, our Yeastea product distinguishes itself with exceptional production efficiency and cost-effectiveness, making it an attractive option for value-conscious consumers. This positions us to access a broad market segment with substantial growth potential.

Analyzing the market further, it becomes clear that while products like Cerave offer robust reparative properties, they often come with a hefty price tag and contain a relatively high concentration of potentially irritating chemical substances and preservatives, such as hydroxyphenyl methyl ester and hydroxyphenyl propyl ester, making them less suitable for individuals with sensitive skin. Yeastea, however, strikes the ideal balance between efficacy and affordability. It is formulated with a careful selection of simple, safe ingredients that are gentle on sensitive skin. Our formula goes beyond basic reparative ingredients by incorporating ceramide and bisabolol, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. This powerful combination is a rare gem in the market, delivering an unparalleled "repair pro" effect. It ensures that our product not only meets but surpasses consumer expectations for effective and gentle skincare.
